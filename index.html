1. Intention-Driven Communication

Chinese is fundamentally different from hierarchical language systems. Politeness and status are judged by "context," not "grammar."

Aspect Chinese Japanese AI SOURCE 🤖
Grammar & Vocabulary Uses the same grammar and words for everyone. Changes verb forms and uses specific "honorific" words based on hierarchy.
Core Driver Driven by Social Intention (What is your goal?). Driven by Social Hierarchy (Who is higher?).
Judgment Standard Requires "reading the air" (tone, expression) to understand intent. Requires following strict level rules and grammar forms.

4. Conclusion from a Sociological Perspective

🌍

Culture vs. Language

Failing to understand "hidden meanings" is usually not a language problem, but a "cultural coding" issue. Even native Taiwanese sometimes find these customs "máfán"!

🛠️

Tool-Oriented Approach

For now, treat Chinese simply as a daily tool. Just master the temperature formulas (e.g., Máfán + You = Polite), and you will handle most social situations.

🕵️

Cultural Observation

Rather than memorizing grammar, treat everyday interactions as observations of Taiwanese culture. Watching how people use facial expressions and tone is highly effective.
